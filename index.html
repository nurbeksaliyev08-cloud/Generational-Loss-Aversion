Generational Loss Aversion in Central Asia

A comprehensive study exploring how Gen Z, Millennials, and Gen X in Central Asia weigh monetary gains vs losses in risky choices, and how historical context shapes financial decision-making.

Take the Survey View Details

Project Overview

This research investigates the psychological phenomenon of loss aversion—the tendency to prefer avoiding losses to acquiring equivalent gains—within the unique socio-economic landscape of Central Asia. By comparing different generational cohorts, we aim to understand how varying levels of exposure to economic transitions and financial shocks influence risk attitudes.

The study focuses on adults aged 18 and older currently residing in Central Asia, categorizing participants into Gen Z, Millennials, and Gen X to identify patterns and shifts in behavioral preferences over time.

Focus

Quantifying loss aversion coefficients (λ) and risk attitudes (α, β) while considering the impact of past financial shocks and entrepreneurial experience.

Population

Targeting a diverse sample of adults across Central Asia, ensuring representation from Gen Z (1997-2012), Millennials (1981-1996), and Gen X (1965-1980).

Format

A structured online survey taking approximately 10–15 minutes, featuring a series of hypothetical mixed gambles and socio-demographic questions.

Methodology

The study utilizes a series of 50/50 mixed gambles compared against sure amounts. This classic behavioral economics approach allows for the estimation of individual parameters for loss aversion and risk sensitivity.

Participants are asked about their birth year, region of residence, experience with financial shocks (e.g., currency devaluations), and entrepreneurial background. All data is collected anonymously and voluntary, with results reported only in aggregate form.

1. Design & Pilot

Development of the survey instrument based on established behavioral models, followed by a pilot phase to ensure clarity and reliability.

2. Data Collection

Wide distribution of the survey across various digital platforms to reach a representative sample of the Central Asian population.

3. Parameter Estimation

Statistical analysis of survey responses to calculate loss aversion and risk preference parameters for each generational group.

4. Visualization & Reporting

Creation of interactive visualizations to communicate findings on how risk behavior varies by age, region, and economic history.
